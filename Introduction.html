Data is collected in the field by taking samples of the pathogens from infected patients, recording the dates on which these sample are taken, sequencing their genomes, and preparing aligned SNP data. These data form the basis for the analysis.

All four methods use exactly the same .fasta files to input the following for each patient (also known as a case or a host):

  • Unique identifier
  • Sample date
  • Aligned genetic sequence

They implement MCMC simulations (whose details vary considerably), and output the following information either in log files or via an R library interface:

  • Simulation number
  • Transmission tree posterior (see 'Discussion' page)
  • Information on transmission events: who infected who
  • Timed phylogenetic trees
The phylogenetic trees, transmission trees and transmission networks, corresponding to sampled simulation results, can be seen on the 'Results' page.
